    Understanding Money Functions

    Introduction

    To avoid economic malfunctions, it's essential to understand money functions. Two significant malfunctions are inflation and deflation.

    Inflation

    • Definition: A general rise in prices.
    • Cause: An increase in demand without a corresponding increase in output, leading to a shortage of goods and services.
    • Effect: Prices rise as people bid against each other or sellers raise their prices due to increased demand.

    Key Points

    • The relationship between the total amount of money and the general price level has been observed for centuries.
    • An increase in the money supply leads to higher prices, unless there is a corresponding increase in goods and services.

    Historical Examples

    • Alexander the Great's spending of captured treasures led to rising prices in Greece.
    • The Spanish removal of gold from their colonies resulted in rising price levels across Europe.
